Community Resources

Wellness services, basic needs assistance, and connection to community resources that support healing, growth, and long-term success for women and children.

Alexandria House connects residents and program participants to a wide range of services — from mental health support and wellness programs to food access, clothing, and referrals to partner organizations throughout Los Angeles.

Wellness Services

Mental Health Support

On-site and referral-based mental health services including individual counseling, trauma-informed therapy, and group support sessions to support emotional healing and resilience.

Health & Wellness

Connections to healthcare providers, reproductive health services, substance use recovery support, and wellness programming that addresses the whole person.

Children's Programs

Age-appropriate enrichment, tutoring, developmental support, and structured activities that help children thrive during times of transition.

Basic Needs Assistance

Food Access

Nutritious meals, pantry support, and connections to food banks and community meal programs ensuring families are nourished and stable.

Clothing & Household Items

Access to donated clothing, household essentials, hygiene products, school supplies, and other daily necessities for women and their children.

Transportation & Navigation

Assistance navigating public transit, accessing transit passes, and coordinating transportation to appointments, employment, schools, and services.

Community Connections

We actively partner with organizations across Los Angeles to connect participants to the broadest possible network of support.

Legal Services

Referrals to legal aid organizations for housing rights, immigration, family law, and protective orders.

Employment Support

Job readiness training, résumé workshops, interview preparation, and connections to hiring partners.

Education & Literacy

GED preparation, ESL classes, financial literacy workshops, and connections to community college programs.

Financial Assistance

Guidance on benefits enrollment, emergency funds, banking access, and long-term financial planning.

Housing Navigation

Support transitioning to permanent housing, including rental assistance referrals and tenant rights education.

Peer Support

Community circles, peer mentorship, and alumni connections that build long-term relationships and accountability.
