Our Approach

Safety is Where Healing Begins

For women and children fleeing domestic violence, homelessness, or trauma, finding a safe place to sleep is not a given — it is a life-changing gift. Alexandria House provides that foundation, offering safe and supportive housing where residents can begin to heal, plan, and grow.

We are intentionally multicultural and anti-racist, committed to addressing the systemic injustices that keep women and families disenfranchised and living in poverty. Our housing programs are not just shelter — they are environments of empowerment, where every resident is treated with respect and given the resources to determine her own future.

Alexandria House

Our original property serves as transitional housing for women and children escaping domestic violence and homelessness. Residents live in a structured, supportive community while working with case managers to stabilize their lives.

The house offers a safe environment free from fear, where mothers can focus on their children's wellbeing and take meaningful steps toward financial and emotional independence.

Expansion House

Permanent Affordable Housing

The Kenmore Residence

The Kenmore Residence is our apartment building offering affordable low-income housing — the permanent destination at the far end of our continuum of care. For residents who have completed transitional programs, Kenmore provides a stable, dignified home they can truly call their own.

By maintaining affordable housing units, Alexandria House helps break the cycle of housing instability and ensures financial hardship does not force families back into homelessness.

Wraparound Support

More Than Four Walls

Safe housing at Alexandria House is never just a roof — it comes with a full network of supportive services designed to help each resident move toward lasting stability. All residents in our transitional housing programs have access to:

  • Individual case management and goal-setting
  • Trauma-informed, culturally responsive support
  • Children's programs and family support
  • Life skills and financial literacy workshops
  • Employment readiness and education support
  • Community wellness and enrichment opportunities
  • Connections to permanent housing resources
  • Basic needs resources for our broader neighborhood
Who We Serve

Seeking Housing Support?

Our transitional housing programs serve women and children who are experiencing homelessness or fleeing domestic violence and trauma. We welcome residents of all backgrounds and are committed to providing an anti-racist, multicultural environment where everyone is treated with dignity.

If you or someone you know is in need of safe housing or crisis support, please reach out to us. Our team will work with you to understand your situation and connect you with the right resources.
