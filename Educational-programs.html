Educational Programs

Empowering women and children through education, entrepreneurship, and enrichment opportunities that build the skills and confidence needed for lasting independence.

Education is central to Alexandria House's mission of empowering women to achieve lasting self-sufficiency. From early childhood care to job development and entrepreneurship training, our programs meet families where they are — and help them reach where they want to go. Many programs are open to residents, participants, and neighbors across the Mid-Wilshire community.

Adult Education & Economic Empowerment

Start-Up Sisterhood LA (SUSLA)

Our flagship 13-week entrepreneurship program gives women the training, mentorship, and resources to launch and grow their own small businesses — from writing a business plan to landing their first client.

See the full program

Job Development

Our Director of Job Development and Entrepreneurship works one-on-one with residents to identify strengths, build career plans, and connect with employers — supporting every step toward economic stability.

Financial Literacy Classes

Interactive workshops on budgeting, saving, credit-building, and banking give participants the financial knowledge they need to achieve and maintain independence long after leaving Alexandria House.

Youth Educational Programs

When mothers pursue work or education, their children need a safe, nurturing place to grow. Alexandria House provides a full continuum of care — from infant programs through school-age enrichment.

Early Childhood Education (ECE)

Our ECE program serves babies and toddlers with a low child-to-staff ratio, providing the individualized attention and developmental support the youngest members of our community need most.

Afterschool Program (K–5)

A free afterschool program for Kindergarten through 5th grade combining enrichment activities, reading development, and tutoring — so children stay on track while their parents pursue their goals.

Day Camp (LAUSD Breaks)

During LAUSD school breaks, our afterschool program becomes a full-day camp with activities and field trips — keeping children in a safe, stimulating environment all year long.

Childcare for Families in Programs

High-quality childcare is available to parents while they work, attend school, or participate in Alexandria House programs — removing a critical barrier to self-sufficiency.

Spotlight: Start-Up Sisterhood LA

SUSLA was born from listening. During COVID-19, many women in our community lost their livelihoods in the service industry. In response, Alexandria House developed this 13-week entrepreneurship program — and today, SUSLA graduates run businesses across Los Angeles. Follow along: @startupsisterhoodla

Business Planning

Participants build a complete business plan from concept to launch, guided step-by-step by instructors and mentors.

Banking & Credit

Workshops on opening business accounts, building credit, and establishing the financial foundation every small business needs.

Website & Marketing

Training in building a web presence and developing marketing strategies to reach customers and grow a brand.

1-on-1 Mentorship

Each participant is paired with a mentor providing personalized support in bookkeeping, accounting, design, and more.

Licensing & Legal Basics

Step-by-step guidance on registering a business, obtaining licenses, and meeting California requirements.

SUSLA Graduate Catering

Many graduates operate their own catering businesses. Support their work through our Top Chef catering vendor directory — contact us to learn more.
